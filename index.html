Popular Cocktails

Mojito

A traditional Cuban highball, that originally served as an effective medicine for Cubans during the 19th century.

Old Fashioned

Developed during the 19th century and given its name during the 1880’s- made by muddling sugar with bitters adding whiskey and a twist of a citrus rind.

Long Island Tea

Invented during a contest in Long Island New York to create a new mixed drink with triple sec in 1972, gets it’s namesake from it’s amber hue.

Negroni

First mixed in Florence, Italy in 1919 when someone asked the bartender to strengthen his favorite cocktail by adding gin instead of the normal soda water.

Whiskey Sour

The oldest historical mention of a whiskey sour was published in the Wisconsin Newspaper in 1870.

Dry Martini

Said to be created when served sometime in the early 1860’s at the Occidental Hotel in the town of “Martin”ez, California.

Daiquiri

Invented by an American mining engineer who was in Cuba at the time of the Spanish-American War

Margarita

Popular Mexican and American drink which became popular during the Prohibition as people drifted over the border for alcohol.

Manhattan

Created during the mid 1870’s for a banquet honoring a presidential candidate in Manhattan, NYC.

Moscow Mule

The mule was born in Manhattan in 1941 when the first carload of Jack Morgan’s Cock n Bull ginger beer was railing over the plains to give New Yorkers a happy surprise.

Cosmopolitan

Created in the 1970’s at a steakhouse in Minneapolis.

Mimosa

Originated in the year 1900 in the Mediterranean and named after the yellow-flowered mimosa plant.
